Chapter 2: Understanding Low Self-Esteem

What is low self-esteem and how does it develop

  • The first step to changing things is understanding or insight
  • In order to make sense of things or work out what direction to go in, you need a map of the territory.
  • Understanding will help you make sense of the thoughts and feelings you have about yourself
  • You will be able to use your map to understand what happens on a day to day basis, and even moment by moment basis
  • Once you understand how the problem works you will be able to see it play out in real time
  • Expecting the worst or putting yourself down, or not allow ing yourself to ask for what you need
  • If you can say here i go again, you wont be emersed in your own thoughts or feelings
  • Just by saying here i go again youre stepping back from them a little, watching them do what they do without feeling you have to engage with them, or get sucked in and do what they say
  • This is like standing next a complex mecahnism watching it work and noticing how the wheels and cogs fit together but not getting caught up in the moving parts
  • You can see what connects to what, whats working as it should be, and also what needs attention and what changes you would like to make
  • Give yourself time and understand its a lot less important where the problem comes from than whats keeping it going
  • Keep observing your thoughts and feelings and behaviour, even if the past is a puzzle to you
  • All the information you need in order for you to find a new kinder more accepting perspective is potenially at your finger tips
  • Whats keeping your low self esteem in the here and now that matters and its in your power to change that now
  • Self esteem is a sense of our own value or worth and its reflected in our attitudes and beliefs about ourself
  • Self esteem can be reflected in using the statement "I am..." and a healthy self esteem would sound something like "I am OK"
  • In contrast low self esteem might sound like "I am not OK" or "I am worthless"
  • These central statements, which reflect beliefs about yourself, the bottom line
  • Because they are the foundations stone of all the thoughts, feelings and actions that make up your day to day experience of self esteem
  • We will examine the bottom line and later find a nicer kinder and more accepting alternative
  • Having a negative bottom line could have a major inpact in your life in all sorts of ways,
  • You might be driving yourself relentlessly, working long hours and wearing yourself out
  • You may avoid challanges thinking that you will fail
  • You do put other people first instead of ignoring your own needs
  • Hide my true self
  • Not everyone is effected to the same extent
  • Some people may be more resilient and able to cope with challenges without their self-esteem being as affected
  • Sometimes their bottom line comes in when there specific situations trigger it, like a job interview
  • Other people may have a more pervasive sense of low self-esteem that affects them in many areas of their life
  • Their bottom lines can be set off by almost anything, and maybe even make them complety cease up, even with something trivial like missing a bus or burning dinner could be enough to make them feel utterly useless
